====== Traps ======

{{ rpd:images:trap_example.png|Trap Example }}

**Traps** are hidden dangers in the dungeon that activate when stepped on or triggered by other means. They can cause various detrimental effects to the hero.

==== Types of Traps ====
  * **[[rpd:poison_trap|Poison Trap]]**: Inflicts poison damage over time
  * **[[rpd:fire_trap|Fire Trap]]**: Deals fire damage and may ignite the victim
  * **[[rpd:alarm_trap|Alarm Trap]]**: Alerts all enemies in the vicinity
  * **[[rpd:lightning_trap|Lightning Trap]]**: Deals electrical damage
  * **[[rpd:gravel_trap|Gravel Trap]]**: Reveals invisible creatures and items
  * **[[rpd:summoning_trap|Summoning Trap]]**: Summons hostile creatures
  * **[[rpd:teleportation_trap|Teleportation Trap]]**: Randomly teleports the hero
  * **[[rpd:paralytic_gas_trap|Paralytic Gas Trap]]**: Paralyzes the hero temporarily
  * **[[rpd:toxic_gas_trap|Toxic Gas Trap]]**: Releases toxic gas in an area

==== Trap Detection ====
  * **Rogue Advantage**: Rogues have a higher chance to detect traps
  * **Awareness**: Some items or abilities enhance trap detection
  * **Visual Clues**: Most traps are hidden but can be spotted with careful attention

==== Trap Avoidance ====
  * **Stealth**: Moving carefully can sometimes avoid triggering traps
  * **Detection Items**: Some items can reveal nearby traps
  * **Safe Movement**: Throwing items or using pets to test dangerous areas

==== Trap Mechanics ====
  * **Activation**: Usually triggered when a character moves onto the trap's cell
  * **Area of Effect**: Some traps affect an area around the trigger point
  * **One-time Use**: Most traps activate once and then become harmless
